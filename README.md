
# ASUS pairing utility for ROG and TUF devices

You can use this utility to pair an ASUS ROG/TUF mouse or keyboard with a separately obtained OEM dongle (from eBay, Aliexpress, etc.). This is useful if you broke/lost your original dongle or bought a product second-hand that didn't include one.


The utility is very finicky It does provide vague instructions on how to pair your device or at least it should.

Based on my limited testing, I was **unable to pair a device's dongle to a different device (One that it was not coded for)**. This suggests that you **cannot reuse a dongle** that was originally intended for a specific peripheral (e.g., a mouse dongle cannot be paired to a different mouse/keyboard).

### Purchasing Replacement Dongles

* You can purchase (supposedly) new replacement dongles from marketplaces like **eBay and AliExpress**.
* **Crucially**, you must ensure the replacement dongle is the **exact one intended for the device you possess**.
* (I am not aware of any known workaround for this.)

---

### Pairing Utility Performance

The pairing **utility can be finicky at times, but it does work**. If the device you are trying to pair to a new dongle is **not listed** in the utility's menu, this typically indicates one of three issues:

1.  **Device Not Supported:** The peripheral you are using may not be supported by the utility or dongle.
2.  **Dongle Mismatch:** The dongle is not correctly coded for the device you are trying to pair. (It's not as easy as looking for a specific model as far as I'm aware they are all the same model which is "ASUSDONGLE".)
3.  **Detection Failure:** The utility has not detected that the peripheral has been plugged in.

#### Troubleshooting Detection Failure

In the case where the peripheral has not been detected, you can usually resolve the issue by:

* **Unplugging and replugging** the device.
* **Restarting** the pairing program.

**All devices are untested except for the ones that are specified as tested**
